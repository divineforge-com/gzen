## Grammar Pattern

**〜わけだ** — "That's why / It means / No wonder"  
**〜わけではない** — "It's not that… / doesn't necessarily mean"  
**〜わけにはいかない** — "Cannot possibly do" (strong prohibition/obligation)

| Form | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| 〜わけだ | logical conclusion / "so that's why" |
| 〜わけではない | partial negation |
| 〜わけがない | impossible, no way |

## Example Sentences

1. 10<ruby>年間<rt>ねんかん</rt></ruby><ruby>日本<rt>にほん</rt></ruby>に<ruby>住<rt>す</rt></ruby>んでいたわけだから、<ruby>日本語<rt>にほんご</rt></ruby>が<ruby>上手<rt>じょうず</rt></ruby>なわけだ。
   *He lived in Japan for 10 years, so no wonder his Japanese is good.*
   　*（繁中：他在日本住了十年，難怪日語這麼好。）*

   *Just because you're busy doesn't mean you can ignore your health.*
   　*（繁中：不能因為忙就忽視健康。）*

3. あんな<ruby>難<rt>むずか</rt></ruby>しい<ruby>試験<rt>しけん</rt></ruby>に<ruby>合格<rt>ごうかく</rt></ruby>できるわけがない。
   *There's no way I can pass such a difficult exam.*
   　*（繁中：那麼難的考試根本不可能通過。）*
