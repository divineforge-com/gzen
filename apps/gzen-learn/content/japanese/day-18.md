## Grammar Pattern

**〜さえ〜ば** — "As long as / if only X, then Y"

Emphasizes that X is the single minimum requirement for Y to happen.

**Structure:** Noun + さえ + Verb (ば-form) / Noun + でさえあれば

> 💡 さえ also appears alone: 〜さえ = "even" (e.g. 子供でさえ知っている = even a child knows)

## Example Sentences

1. <ruby>お金<rt>おかね</rt></ruby>さえあれば、<ruby>何<rt>なん</rt></ruby>でもできる。
   *If only I had money, I could do anything.*
　*（繁中：只要有錢，什麼都做得到。）*

2. <ruby>天気<rt>てんき</rt></ruby>さえよければ、<ruby>旅行<rt>りょこう</rt></ruby>は<ruby>完璧<rt>かんぺき</rt></ruby>だったのに。
   *If only the weather had been good, the trip would have been perfect.*
　*（繁中：只要天氣好，旅行就完美了。）*

3. あなたさえいれば、<ruby>他<rt>ほか</rt></ruby>には<ruby>何<rt>なに</rt></ruby>もいらない。
   *As long as I have you, I need nothing else.*
　*（繁中：只要有你，其他什麼都不需要。）*
