## Russian Cases Overview

Russian has **6 cases**. The case of a noun changes its ending based on its role in the sentence.

| Case | Question | Pronunciation | Role |
|------|----------|--------------|------|
| Nominative | кто? что? | kto? shto? | Subject |
| Accusative | кого? что? | ka-VO? shto? | Direct object |
| Genitive | кого? чего? | ka-VO? chye-VO? | Possession / absence |
| Dative | кому? чему? | ka-MOO? chye-MOO? | Indirect object |
| Instrumental | кем? чем? | kyem? chyem? | With / by means of |
| Prepositional | о ком? о чём? | a kom? a chyom? | About / in |

## Nominative Case (Именительный падеж)

The basic, "dictionary" form. Used for the subject of the sentence.

- **Кот** спит. *(kot spit)* *(The cat is sleeping.)*
- **Анна** читает книгу. *(AH-na chi-TA-yet KNEEgoo)* *(Anna is reading a book.)*

## Accusative Case (Винительный падеж)

Used for the **direct object** — what the action is done to.

| Gender | Nominative | Accusative |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Masculine (inanimate) | стол | стол (no change) |
| Masculine (animate) | студент | студент**а** |
| Feminine | книга | книг**у** |
| Neuter | окно | окно (no change) |

> 💡 **Animate vs inanimate** matters for masculine nouns! People/animals change; objects don't.

## Examples

- Я вижу **стол**. *(ya VEE-zhoo stol)* *(I see the table.)* — inanimate, no change
- Я вижу **студента**. *(ya VEE-zhoo stoo-DYEN-ta)* *(I see the student.)* — animate, -а ending
- Я читаю **книгу**. *(ya chi-TA-yu KNEEgoo)* *(I'm reading a book.)* — feminine, -у ending
