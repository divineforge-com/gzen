## The Two Aspects

Every Russian verb comes in two flavors:

| | **Imperfective (НСВ)** | **Perfective (СВ)** |
|---|----------------------|-------------------|
| Focus | process, habit, ongoing | completed action, result |
| Tenses | past, present, future | past and future only |
| Question | что делать? | что сделать? |

## Common Aspect Pairs

| Imperfective | Pronunciation | Perfective | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|-------------|--------------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| читать | chi-TAT' | прочитать | pra-chi-TAT' | to read |
| писать | pi-SAT' | написать | na-pi-SAT' | to write |
| делать | DYE-lat' | сделать | ZDYE-lat' | to do/make |
| говорить | ga-va-RIT' | сказать | ska-ZAT' | to say/speak |
| покупать | pa-koo-PAT' | купить | koo-PIT' | to buy |
| учить | oo-CHIT' | выучить | VY-oo-chit' | to learn |
| смотреть | smat-RYET' | посмотреть | pas-mat-RYET' | to watch |
| помогать | pa-ma-GAT' | помочь | pa-MOCH' | to help |

## When to Use Which

**Imperfective** (process/habit):
- Я **читал** книгу весь день. *(ya chi-TAL KNEEgoo vyes' dyen')* *(I was reading the book all day.)*
- Он **читает** каждый вечер. *(on chi-TA-yet KAZHdiy VYEcher)* *(He reads every evening.)*

**Perfective** (completed):
- Я **прочитал** книгу. *(ya pra-chi-TAL KNEEgoo)* *(I read / finished the book.)*
- Завтра я **прочитаю** эту статью. *(ZAFtra ya pra-chi-TA-yu EH-too stat'-YU)* *(Tomorrow I will read / finish this article.)*

> 💡 Perfective has **no present tense** — a perfective verb in present form becomes future!
