## Past Tense Formation

Russian past tense is simple: remove -ть from infinitive, add gender ending.

| Gender | Ending | Example (читать) | Pronunciation |
|--------|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| Masculine | -л | он читал | on chi-TAL |
| Feminine | -ла | она читала | a-NA chi-TA-la |
| Neuter | -ло | оно читало | a-NO chi-TA-la |
| Plural | -ли | они читали | a-NI chi-TA-li |

> 💡 Past tense agrees with **gender of subject**, NOT person. Я (I) uses -л if speaker is male, -ла if female!

## Examples

- Он **читал** газету. *(on chi-TAL ga-ZYE-too)* *(He was reading the newspaper.)*
- Она **читала** книгу. *(a-NA chi-TA-la KNEEgoo)* *(She was reading a book.)*
- **Я читал** *(ya chi-TAL)* (male speaker) / **Я читала** *(ya chi-TA-la)* (female speaker) роман *(ra-MAN)*.

## Aspect in Past Tense

| | Imperfective past | Perfective past |
|---|------------------|----------------|
| Meaning | was doing / used to | did (completed) |
| Example | Я **читал** — I was reading | Я **прочитал** — I read (finished it) |
