## Personal Pronouns (Nominative Case)

| Russian | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| я | ya | I |
| ты | ty | you (informal, singular) |
| он | on | he / it (masc.) |
| она | a-NA | she / it (fem.) |
| оно | a-NO | it (neuter) |
| мы | my | we |
| вы | vy | you (formal/plural) |
| они | a-NI | they |

> 💡 **Вы** (capital V) is the formal "you" — used with strangers, elders, and in professional settings. **ты** is for friends, family, and children.

## Pronouns in Sentences

Russian often **drops the pronoun** when the subject is clear from context (like Spanish/Italian):

- Я читаю (ya chi-TA-yu). → Читаю (chi-TA-yu). *(I read / I'm reading)*

## Possessive Pronouns

| Russian | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| мой / моя / моё / мои | moy / ma-YA / ma-YO / ma-EE | my (m/f/n/pl) |
| твой / твоя / твоё / твои | tvoy / tva-YA / tva-YO / tva-EE | your (informal) |
| его | ye-VO | his / its |
| её | ye-YO | her / its |
| наш / наша / наше / наши | nash / NA-sha / NA-she / NA-shi | our |
| ваш / ваша / ваше / ваши | vash / VA-sha / VA-she / VA-shi | your (formal/plural) |
| их | ikh | their |
