## Observation

We live in an era that cannot stop. Phone screens blink with notifications, news feeds update endlessly, and work emails intrude into personal spaces at any hour. Even in our leisure time, our minds are filled with plans, worries, and unfinished conversations.

When external information pours in like a flood, our minds become fragmented and anxious. We instinctively react to every stimulus, losing our internal center—like a leaf blown about in a storm. We feel exhausted, yet we do not know how to bring things to a halt.

---

## Principle

The I Ching says regarding Hexagram Gèn: \"Keeping still at the back, one does not feel one's body; walking in the courtyard, one does not see the people. There is no blame.\"
The image of Hexagram **Gèn** is the **Mountain**, representing **stillness and stopping**.

\"Keeping still at the back\" is a deep metaphor for spiritual practice: when the spine is upright and quiet, one transcends the sensory desires of the ego (\"one does not feel one's body\"). When one abides in this unshakeable stillness, even when moving through a busy courtyard, one remains undisturbed by external affairs (\"one does not see the people\").

This is identical to Zen **Zazen (seated meditation)** and Buddhist **Samatha (calm abiding / stopping)**. A mountain does not move because of a strong wind, nor does it become anxious as seasons change. It simply sits, holding all things, watching the clouds gather and disperse with equanimity.

The wisdom of Gèn teaches us that stillness is not passive escape; it is a source of immense spiritual power. Only when the turbulent surface of a lake settles can we see the stones at the bottom. Only when scattered thoughts quiet down can we regain clarity.

---

## Application

You can apply the mountain-like stillness of Hexagram Gèn in daily life through three steps:

1. **Sit Like a Mountain (Gèn-Back Meditation)**
   Set aside 10 to 15 minutes each day to sit quietly.
   - Keep your spine upright (the physical practice of \"keeping still at the back\"), placing your hands in the cosmic mudra on your lap.
   - Focus your attention on your breath, or simply sit.
   - As thoughts (notifications, worries, plans) arise like mist, do not chase them and do not fight them. Like a mountain, let the mist float around you while your foundation remains unmoved.

2. **Practice "Intentionally Stopping" in Daily Life**
   - **Digital Silence**: Dedicate one hour each day as your \"Gèn-time\" (stillness time). Turn off your phone and computer. Receive no external inputs, letting your brain rest fully.
   - **Pause Before Reacting**: When you see an upsetting comment online or receive an offensive email, do not reply immediately. Close your eyes, take three deep breaths, and build a \"mountain\" in your mind. Let the waves of emotion crash against the stone and dissolve naturally. Only then respond with a clear mind.

3. **Simplify Your Life Dimensions**
   The wisdom of Gèn requires subtraction. Ask yourself: What unnecessary burdens am I carrying in my life and work? Learn to say no and learn to stop. In taking a step back, we often see things much more clearly.
